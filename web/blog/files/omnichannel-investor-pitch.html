Investor brief · Confidential

The control layer
for enterprise AI.

AI does the work. People stay accountable. That gap is the bottleneck, and the business.

Enterprises are not short on AI capability. They are short on a way to deploy it that a board, a regulator, and a customer can all stand behind. We build that layer, and we are already paid to install the thinking behind it.

Why now

Capability outran permission.

The models can already do the work. What has not arrived is the willingness to let them act unsupervised against real customers and real money. The blocker on enterprise AI spend is not capability, it is accountability. Pilots stall at the exact moment someone has to put their name on autonomous action.

Whoever resolves the accountability problem unlocks the budget that is currently frozen in pilot purgatory. We think the resolution is structural, not a better model: a layer where AI proposes and a human with authority decides, with the proof captured by construction.

The insight

We sell the dial that lets enterprises say yes.

Human in control is not a feature, it is the architecture. AI never holds execution credentials. Every consequential action passes a gate where a human approves, and the threshold for what needs a human is a dial the customer owns and widens as trust is earned. That is what turns a stalled pilot into a deployment. We are not selling autonomy. We are selling defensible adoption.

What we are building

A work operating system, sold on outcomes.

Today: advisory, with revenue

A Maturity Review and operating-model design across an organisation's work, front office and back. This funds the company now and writes the procedures the product will run on. Every engagement is also discovery for the platform.

Tomorrow: the platform

A composable work-orchestration layer. AI proposes, people approve at thresholds they set, the system executes and audits by construction. Priced on outcomes, work completed and approvals cleared, not seats.

Why we win

Operators, not whiteboarders.

Operational depth

Years running workload management across contact centre and back office. We know how work behaves under load. Most AI-for-operations entrants have never sized a queue.

Govern, do not just execute

The architecture can govern agents that run inside other platforms. That makes us a control layer over the whole stack, not another silo competing with the CRM and the ERP.

Aligned pricing

Outcome-based pricing ties our revenue to the customer's value, not their headcount. It is the model a CFO trusts and a competitor on seat licences cannot easily match.

Market

Every repeatable process is in scope.

The work is not confined to a department. We start in regulated, high-volume operations: banking, insurance, telecoms, and utilities, where the cost of an unaccountable AI decision is highest and the audit requirement is non-negotiable. From that beachhead we expand across the rest of the organisation under one governance layer.
